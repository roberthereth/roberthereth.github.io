Spring can be categorized by three seasonings: basil, oregano, and chives. The flavors of spring flow from the warmpth of wintertime meals to the cooling effects of a summertime snack and everywhere inbetween. Though often overlooked, spring's spices and herbs act as a gateway between seasons, offering a refreshing transition between cold and warmpth. Greens tend to be everpresent in spring's assortment of seasonings, aided by its right hand man: garlic. Spring's flavors tend to be pungent and earthy with varying levels of spice, salt, sweet, and savory.

Though spring's meals aren't truly its own, the main three herbs add a boatload of flavour to whatever savory dish they come in contact with. Breakfast's omlettes and sausage would be incomplete without the presence of chive and oregano. Lunch's light salads and vegan offerings use basil to boost the flavor and scent of their dish. Dinners would be incomplete without the presence of these three through braised stews, grilled meats, and vegetable sides.
